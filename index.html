Italy's most important city, Venice.

By Ekansh Simgekar

Venice is a city in northeastern Italy, being the capital of the Veneto region. It was built on a group of 127 islands separated by water and connected by canals. It is considered nowadays to be metropolitan area, with a population of 2.6 million. The name comes from the Veneti people who were the first original inhabitants of the land, developing the economy and land into a prosperous place. However, Napoleons conquest led it to fall and be taken under the control of Italy. Most of the city and its lagoon are UNESCO World Heritage Sites, due to its artisitic and musical influence. It remains a amazingly popular tourist destination being described by many like The Times and The New York Times as the most bueatiful city built by man.
